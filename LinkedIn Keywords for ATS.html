Where LinkedIn keywords matter most

Where LinkedIn keywords matter most

Recruiter search and LinkedIn's own algorithm weigh certain profile sections more heavily than others.

01

Headline carries the most search weight

Your headline is one of the most heavily indexed fields — include your target role and top skill directly.

02

Skills section drives endorsement-based search

Recruiters often filter by specific skills — list the exact terms used in job postings you're targeting.

03

About section supports long-tail search terms

A keyword-rich but natural About section improves your visibility for broader, related search terms too.

Checklist

LinkedIn keyword placement checklist

Confirm keywords appear naturally in each of these sections.

Target role/title in headline
Top 3-5 skills in headline or About
Skills section fully filled (up to 50)
Keywords repeated naturally in experience bullets
Industry-standard job titles used
Location and open-to-work settings updated
Certifications section filled if relevant
About section written in first person, keyword-rich

Mistakes

Common LinkedIn keyword mistakes

✕

Vague headlines with no role or skill

"Passionate professional" contains nothing recruiters actually search for — always name your role and top skills.

✕

Leaving the skills section mostly empty

An incomplete skills section significantly limits how often you appear in recruiter searches.

✕

Keyword stuffing unnaturally

Repeating keywords excessively in the About section can look spammy — use them naturally within real sentences.

Pro tips

Pro tips for LinkedIn keyword optimization

Mirror real job posting language

Use the exact role titles and skill terms from job postings you're targeting.

Fill every relevant skill slot

LinkedIn allows up to 50 skills — use as many genuinely relevant ones as possible.

Update keywords as your goals shift

Revisit your headline and skills whenever you're targeting a new type of role.

FAQ

LinkedIn keywords for ATS — FAQ

Does LinkedIn use ATS-style keyword matching?

Yes — LinkedIn Recruiter and internal search tools use keyword matching similar in principle to traditional ATS software.

Where should I place my most important keywords?

Your headline carries the most search weight, followed by your skills section and About summary.

How many skills should I list on LinkedIn?

Fill as many of the available skill slots (up to 50) as are genuinely relevant to maximize search visibility.
