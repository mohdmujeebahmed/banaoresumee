→ What recruiters expect from a final-year resume

What recruiters expect from a final-year resume

Recruiters know final-year candidates are close to job-ready — the resume needs to show that clearly.

01

A strong final-year/capstone project

This is often the single most important line on a final-year resume.

02

Internship experience, even short-term

Any relevant internship signals real-world readiness beyond the classroom.

03

Clear graduation timeline

Recruiters need to know exactly when you'll be available to join full-time.

✕

Underselling the final year project

→ Pro tips

Pro tips for final-year students

Highlight the capstone project first

For most final-year students, this project carries more weight than older coursework.

List all relevant internships

Even short or unpaid internships count as valuable proof of real-world exposure.

Prepare a placement-ready one-pager

Keep the format extremely clean for automated campus placement portals.

→ FAQ

Final year student resume — FAQ

What should a final-year resume emphasize?

Your capstone/final-year project, any internships, and a clear expected graduation date.

Should I include my CGPA?

Include it if it's 3.3+ (or ~7.5/10) — otherwise, let your project and skills speak instead.

Is this different from a general fresher resume?

It's similar, but should emphasize the final-year project more heavily since it's usually your strongest asset.
