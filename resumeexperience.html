→ What recruiters look for in your experience

What recruiters look for in your experience

Recruiters spend the most time on this section — each bullet needs to show impact, not just duty.

01

Bullets that start with strong verbs

"Responsible for" is weak. "Led," "built," "reduced" show ownership and impact instead.

02

A measurable result in most bullets

Numbers — percentages, revenue, time saved — make impact concrete and memorable.

03

Relevance to the role you're applying for

Reorder or trim bullets so the most job-relevant experience appears first.

→ Mistakes

Common resume experience mistakes

✕

Using "responsible for" or "duties included"

These phrases describe a job description, not your impact — replace with action verbs and results.

✕

No metrics anywhere in the section

Even an estimated number is stronger than a metric-free bullet — quantify wherever possible.

✕

Listing every task instead of the top 3-5

Recruiters don't need a full job description — pick your 3-5 most impressive, relevant bullets per role.

→ Pro tips

Pro tips for the experience section

Use the XYZ formula

"Accomplished X, measured by Y, by doing Z" — a reliable structure for any bullet.

Tailor bullet order per job

Move your most relevant bullet to the top of each role for the specific job you're applying to.

Quantify even small wins

"Helped 10+ customers daily" is still a number — don't skip metrics just because they're modest.

→ FAQ

Resume experience — FAQ

How do I write resume bullet points?

Start each bullet with a strong action verb, describe what you did, and close with a measurable result wherever possible.

Should freshers include internships as experience?

Yes — internships, part-time jobs, and even significant volunteer work count as valid experience for freshers.

How many bullets per job should I include?

3-5 bullets per role is standard — prioritize the most relevant and impactful ones for the job you want.
