Mistakes

Common thank you email mistakes

✕

Sending a generic, copy-pasted note

Always include at least one specific detail from your actual conversation.

✕

Waiting several days to send it

A thank you email loses much of its impact if sent more than a day or two after the interview.

✕

Sending one identical email to multiple interviewers together

When possible, send individual notes with slight personalization to each interviewer.

Pro tips

Pro tips for interview thank you emails

Send it the same day if possible

Same-day thank you notes consistently leave the strongest impression.

Mention something specific from the conversation

This shows genuine engagement rather than a generic, templated response.

Keep the tone warm but professional

A sincere, human tone reads better than an overly formal or stiff message.

FAQ

Thank you email after interview — FAQ

Is a thank you email necessary after every interview?

It's not strictly required, but it's a simple, low-effort gesture that consistently leaves a positive, professional impression.

What's the difference between a thank you and a follow-up email?

A thank you email is sent immediately to express gratitude; a follow-up is typically sent later to check on application status.

Should I send separate emails to multiple interviewers?

Yes, when possible — individual, slightly personalized notes are stronger than one group email.
