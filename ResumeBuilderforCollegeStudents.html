Interview prep 🎙

Common interview questions for new grads

Scroll — these are the questions students say come up most in first-round campus interviews.

"Tell me about yourself"

Lead with your major and one project or internship, not your hometown. 60 seconds, max.

"Why this company?"

Name one specific product, team, or value — generic answers ("great culture") get filtered out mentally.

"Tell me about a time you failed"

Pick a real, small failure. Spend most of the answer on what you changed after, not the failure itself.

"Do you have questions for us?"

Always say yes. Ask about team structure or what success looks like in 90 days — never ask about pay first.

Behavioral / STAR

Situation, Task, Action, Result — in that order, out loud, before you ever walk into the room.

Frequently asked

Questions students ask before applying

How long should a college student's resume be?

One page. Always. Recruiters reading hundreds of student resumes will not read a second page, no matter how strong your GPA or project list is.

Do I need work experience to build a strong resume?

No. Class projects, group case competitions, clubs, and campus jobs all count as experience if you describe them with a result, not just a task list.

Should I include my GPA?

Include it if it's 3.3 or higher, or if the employer specifically asks. Otherwise, use that space for a project or skill instead.

What file format do recruiters actually prefer?

PDF, unless the application system explicitly asks for a Word doc — some older ATS platforms parse .docx more reliably than PDF.
