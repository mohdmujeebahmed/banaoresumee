SECTION 01 / WHY THIS MATTERS

Why 90% of Fresher Resumes Get Auto‑Rejected

Here's the part nobody tells you in college: your resume isn't read by a human first. It's scanned by an Applicant Tracking System (ATS) — software that filters resumes before a recruiter ever opens them.

If your resume has a creative "artistic" font, a photo, a QR code, a "Kindly Consider Me" objective paragraph from 2009, or a two-column layout that confuses the parser — it's probably getting filtered into the void before a human even sees it.

This isn't fearmongering. It's just how hiring works at scale. Companies receiving 200+ applications per junior role need automated filtering, and freshers consistently get caught in it because nobody taught them the rules of the game.

The good news? Once you know the rules, you can build a resume that wins on both fronts — robot and human.

SECTION 02 / THE GATEKEEPER

The ATS Monster: What It Actually Wants

Think of the ATS like a very literal, very humorless librarian. It doesn't care about your vibe. It cares about structure.

ATS LovesATS Hates
Standard headings: Experience, Education, SkillsCreative headings: "My Journey", "What I Bring"
.docx or text-based PDFHeavily designed PDFs with graphics-as-text
Reverse chronological orderRandom or "creative" ordering
Keywords lifted from the job descriptionVague synonyms instead of the JD's exact terms
Standard fonts: Calibri, Arial, GeorgiaDecorative or script fonts
Single column layoutMulti-column or sidebar layouts

Rule of thumb: if you'd struggle to copy-paste the text out of your resume cleanly into Notepad, the ATS will struggle to read it too. Simplicity isn't boring here — it's strategic.

SECTION 03 / STRUCTURE

The Anatomy of a Resume That Doesn't Suck

Forget "objective statements." Forget centering your name in 40pt Old English font. Here's the structure that consistently performs for entry-level candidates.

1. Header — keep it boring, keep it clean

Name, phone number, professional email (not coolboy_rahul99@yahoo.com), city, LinkedIn URL, GitHub/portfolio if relevant. That's it.

2. Professional summary — 2–3 lines, not a paragraph

Skip "Seeking an opportunity to utilize my skills." Recruiters care what you can do:

"Computer Science graduate with hands-on experience in Python, SQL, and React through 3 academic projects and a 2-month internship at [Company]. Built a full-stack inventory system used by 40+ test users. Looking to bring strong problem-solving and quick-learning ability to an entry-level software role."

3. Skills — match the job description, literally

Split into Technical Skills and Soft Skills. Pull exact phrases from the posting — if it says "cross-functional collaboration," use that exact phrase if it's true for you.

4. Education

GPA/percentage only if it's strong (7.5+ CGPA or 75%+). Mention relevant coursework if your degree is unrelated to the role.

5. Projects — your secret weapon as a fresher

This is the section that wins you the interview when you have zero work experience. More on this in Section 05.

6. Internships / Experience

Even a 2-week unpaid internship counts. Frame it with results, not duties.

7. Certifications

Coursera, Google, freeCodeCamp, NPTEL — all valid if relevant to the role.

SECTION 04 / CONFESSIONS

Resume Sins Freshers Commit (And How to Repent)

Let's roast some classics, gently, with love.

SIN 01 "Hardworking, dedicated, punctual" as your top skills

Everyone claims this. Recruiters have started physically flinching.

↳ Repentance

Replace with measurable skills: "Proficient in Excel (VLOOKUP, Pivot Tables), Python (Pandas, NumPy), SQL."

SIN 02 A 3-page resume with zero work experience

If it's not one page, you're including filler.

↳ Repentance

One page. Always one page for freshers. No exceptions.

SIN 03 Listing "Microsoft Word" as a skill

It's 2026. Knowing how to use a browser is a prerequisite for being alive, not a skill.

↳ Repentance

Swap for tools that actually differentiate you — specific software, languages, frameworks.

SIN 04 A generic objective that fits any job, anywhere

If you send the same resume to a marketing internship and a data analyst role, you're doing it wrong.

↳ Repentance

Tailor it. Every. Single. Time.

SIN 05 No quantifiable results, anywhere

"Improved app load time" means nothing on its own.

↳ Repentance

"Improved app load time by 35% through code splitting" means everything.

SECTION 05 / THE BIG PANIC

The "No Experience" Problem — Solved

This is the #1 panic point for freshers: "I have no work experience, what do I even put on this thing?"

Here's the secret: recruiters hiring freshers don't expect work experience. They expect potential. So show it through:

  • Academic projects — treat your final-year project like a real product. What problem did it solve? What stack? What was the outcome?
  • Hackathons — even without winning, participation shows initiative. "Built a disaster-alert app in 36 hours, placed top 10 of 80 teams."
  • Freelance/volunteer work — made a poster for a college fest? That's graphic design. Ran your hostel's Instagram? That's social media management.
  • Open source contributions — even small PRs on GitHub show real-world coding behavior.
  • Online courses with projects — a completed Coursera/Udemy capstone is legitimate proof of skill.

The trick is framing, not fabricating. You did more than you think — you just never called it "experience."

SECTION 06 / VOCABULARY

Action Verbs That Don't Make Recruiters Cringe

Swap the tired ones for these:

Instead of…Use…
"Worked on"Engineered, Developed, Built, Implemented
"Helped with"Collaborated, Contributed, Co-led
"Responsible for"Owned, Managed, Drove
"Did research"Analyzed, Investigated, Benchmarked
"Made better"Optimized, Streamlined, Enhanced

Pair every action verb with a number or outcome wherever you can. "Engineered a Python script that automated data entry, saving 5 hours/week" hits ten times harder than "Worked on automation."

SECTION 08 / FAQ

FAQs Every Fresher Asks

Should my fresher resume be one page or can it be two?+

One page. Always. Unless you have 5+ years of internships, research papers, and certifications combined, two pages reads as padding.

Do I need a photo on my resume?+

No — not for India, US, UK, or most global markets. Photos can trigger unconscious bias filters, and most ATS systems don't parse images anyway.

What file format should I submit — PDF or Word?+

PDF, unless the job posting specifically asks for Word (.docx). PDF preserves formatting — just make sure it's text-based, not a scanned image.

How many skills should I list?+

8–12 relevant skills is the sweet spot. More than that looks like keyword stuffing; fewer looks underprepared.

Should I include my CGPA if it's average?+

If it's below 7/10 or 70%, you can omit it and let your projects and skills speak instead. If asked directly in an application form, always be honest there.

Is it okay to use a resume template found online?+

Yes, as long as it's a single-column, ATS-friendly format. Avoid heavily graphic templates from generic template sites — they often break ATS parsing.
