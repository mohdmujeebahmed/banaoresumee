01What even is an ATS, and why is it ghosting you?

An ATS is software that scans, sorts, and ranks resumes before a recruiter looks at them. It's looking for specific keywords, clean formatting, and structure it can actually parse. Tables, text boxes, headshots, weird fonts, columns — all of that confuses it, and confused ATS = instant reject pile.

This is exactly the gap an AI resume builder closes. Instead of guessing what formatting is "safe," the AI builds it ATS-compatible from the start — while still looking genuinely good to the human who eventually opens it.

04Mistakes that are quietly killing your callback rate

Mistake

"To work in a reputed company and grow professionally" as your objective.

Fix: Recruiters skip generic objectives entirely. Replace with a 2-line summary of what you actually bring.
Mistake

Listing duties instead of results: "Handled social media accounts."

Fix: Add numbers. "Grew Instagram engagement 35% across 3 client accounts in 6 months."
Mistake

Sending the exact same resume to every single job.

Fix: Tweak keywords to match each job description — it takes 2 minutes and matters a lot to the ATS.
Mistake

Tables, columns, or a photo on a private-sector resume.

Fix: Keep it single-column and text-based unless it's a govt application that explicitly asks for a photo.
