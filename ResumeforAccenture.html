What recruiters expect

Accenture recruiters read for client impact, not just duties

Whether it's an analyst, consultant, or technology role, Accenture recruiters and its ATS are scanning for evidence you can deliver results for a client, not just a description of what your job involved.

Client and business outcomes

Bullets framed around what changed for a client or business — cost saved, revenue driven, timeline hit — read stronger than task lists.

Cross-functional collaboration

Consulting work is team-based by nature. Recruiters look for language showing you worked across teams, stakeholders, or disciplines.

Exact keyword matches

Accenture's ATS ranks resumes by overlap with the job posting's language — tools, methodologies, and certifications need to match, not just imply.

Common mistakes

What gets Accenture resumes filtered out

  • ✕Tables, columns, or graphics. Many ATS parsers misread multi-column layouts and drop entire sections of text.
  • ✕Task lists instead of outcomes. "Responsible for client reporting" gives the system nothing to match against the posting.
  • ✕Certifications left off. Scrum, PMP, or platform certifications omitted entirely, even when the applicant holds them.
  • ✕Generic job titles. "Team member" instead of the posting's actual title reduces keyword match score.
  • ✕No business impact. Bullets without a measurable outcome read as unverifiable and get skipped in a fast recruiter scan.
Interview tips

Once your resume gets through, prep for this

01

Expect case-style questions

Consulting interviews often include a business case or problem-solving scenario. Practice structuring an answer out loud, not just in your head.

02

Prepare STAR stories

Have 3-4 stories ready in Situation, Task, Action, Result format that show client impact, teamwork, and handling ambiguity.

03

Research the client industries

Accenture interviewers often ask why you're interested in a specific industry group — retail, financial services, health, etc. Have a real answer ready.

04

Ask about team structure

Project staffing and travel expectations vary widely. Clarify how teams are structured and staffed before you're deep into the process.
