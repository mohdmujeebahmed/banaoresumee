ATS resume checklist ✓

Is your resume ATS friendly?

Every applicant tracking system checker looks for these fundamentals. Check yours before you hit submit.

Standard section headings (Experience, Education, Skills)
No tables, columns, or text boxes
Keywords from the job description included naturally
Saved as .docx or text-based PDF, not an image
Simple, ATS-readable fonts (Arial, Calibri, Times New Roman)
No headers/footers hiding contact information
Consistent date formatting throughout
File named professionally (FirstName-LastName-Resume)

Common ATS mistakes ✕

Why your resume gets rejected by ATS

✕

Resume submitted as an image or scanned PDF

Most applicant tracking systems can't read image-based resumes. Always export as text-based PDF or .docx.

✕

Keywords missing from the job description

An ATS resume scanner ranks resumes by keyword match rate — copy exact skill and title phrases from the posting.

✕

Tables, columns, or graphics in the layout

Multi-column resumes confuse ATS parsers and scramble your work history — keep it single-column.

✕

Non-standard section titles

"My Journey" instead of "Experience" won't be recognized. Stick to standard ATS resume headings.
