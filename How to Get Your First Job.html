A realistic first-job timeline

What the first-job search typically looks like, week by week.

Weeks 1-2Build resume, LinkedIn, and portfolio/project links. Identify 30-40 target companies or roles.
Weeks 3-6Apply to 10-15 roles weekly, tailored per posting. Reach out to 3-5 people weekly for informational chats.
Weeks 6+Interview, follow up within 24 hours of each round, and keep applying until you have a signed offer.
Mistakes

Common first-job search mistakes

✕

Waiting to "gain more experience" before applying

Most entry-level roles expect limited experience — apply as soon as your resume is solid.

✕

Mass-applying with one generic resume

Tailoring even slightly to each job significantly improves response rates.

✕

Skipping networking entirely

A large percentage of first jobs come through referrals or warm introductions, not cold applications alone.

Pro tips

Pro tips for landing your first job

Reach out before you need to

Message alumni or professionals in your target field weeks before applying, not the day of.

Follow up every application

A short follow-up email after a week shows initiative and keeps you visible.

Treat rejection as data

Track what stage you're getting rejected at — resume, interview, or offer — and adjust that specific step.

FAQ

How to get your first job — FAQ

How do I get a job with zero work experience?

Lead with projects, internships, volunteering, and certifications — these all count as valid proof of applied skill.

How many jobs should I apply to per week?

Aim for 10-15 well-matched, tailored applications weekly rather than mass-applying to hundreds of unrelated roles.

Does networking really help for a first job?

Yes — many entry-level roles are filled through referrals, so reaching out to your network early makes a real difference.
