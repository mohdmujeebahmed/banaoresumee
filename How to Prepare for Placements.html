What campus placements actually test

What campus placements actually test

Placement drives typically test across four stages — preparing for each separately improves your odds significantly.

01

Resume screening (often via ATS portal)

A clean, ATS-safe resume with CGPA, branch, and skills clearly visible is the first filter.

02

Aptitude and technical rounds

Quantitative aptitude, logical reasoning, and core subject questions are standard across most drives.

03

HR and behavioral interviews

Companies assess communication, cultural fit, and clarity about your career goals in this final stage.

Examples

A 4-week placement prep plan

A realistic breakdown for the month before placement season begins.

Week 1Finalize resume and LinkedIn. Start daily aptitude practice (30-45 mins).
Week 2Revise core technical subjects relevant to your branch. Continue aptitude practice.
Week 3Do 2-3 mock technical interviews and 1-2 mock HR interviews with peers or mentors.
Week 4Research target companies, refine answers to common questions, and rest before the drive begins.
Mistakes

Common placement preparation mistakes

✕

Starting technical revision too late

Core subject revision needs consistent practice over weeks, not a last-minute cram.

✕

Ignoring the resume until the last moment

Many placement portals scan resumes via ATS — a rushed resume can fail before interviews even begin.

✕

Skipping mock interviews entirely

Practicing out loud with a peer significantly improves clarity and confidence over silent preparation alone.

Pro tips

Pro tips for placement season

Practice explaining your final year project

This is one of the most commonly asked questions across nearly every placement interview.

Research each company briefly before interviews

Knowing basic company context signals genuine interest to interviewers.

Keep a placement tracker

Track applications, rounds cleared, and feedback to identify where you need more preparation.

FAQ

Placement preparation — FAQ

How early should I start placement preparation?

Ideally 4-6 weeks before placement season begins, covering resume, aptitude, technical, and interview practice in that order.

What's most commonly asked in placement interviews?

Your final year project, core technical fundamentals, and standard HR questions like strengths, weaknesses, and career goals.

Do I need a different resume for each company?

A master resume is enough for most drives, though minor tailoring of top skills per company can help.
