What recruiters look for in an accountant resume

Recruiters hiring accountants scan for specific software, compliance knowledge, and accuracy at scale.

01

Accounting software named clearly

Tally, QuickBooks, SAP, or Zoho Books — specific tools matter more than "accounting software."

02

Compliance and taxation knowledge

03

Volume and accuracy quantified

→ Mistakes

Common accountant resume mistakes

✕

Listing "accounting software" without naming it

Specify Tally, QuickBooks, or SAP by name for both ATS and recruiter clarity.

✕

No mention of compliance work

GST, TDS, or audit exposure is often expected — leaving it out weakens the resume.

✕

No accuracy or volume metrics

"Managed accounts" is weaker than "reconciled 500+ transactions with 99.8% accuracy."

→ Pro tips

Pro tips for accountant resumes

Quantify accuracy and volume

Numbers build trust in a field where precision is the core job requirement.

List every software you've used

Recruiters often filter by specific software proficiency.

Highlight compliance deadlines met

Meeting GST/TDS deadlines consistently is a strong, quantifiable achievement.

→ FAQ

Accountant resume — FAQ

What skills should an accountant resume include?

Accounting software (Tally/QuickBooks), GST/TDS knowledge, reconciliation, and Excel proficiency are core expectations.

Do I need certifications listed on an accountant resume?

Yes, if you hold any — CA, CMA, or relevant certifications should be prominently listed.

What's the ideal length for an accountant resume?

One page for early-career accountants, up to two pages for senior or specialized roles.
