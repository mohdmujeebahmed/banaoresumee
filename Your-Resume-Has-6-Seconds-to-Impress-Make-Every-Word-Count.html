6 Rules to Pass Every ATS ↓

🔑

Mirror Job Description Keywords

ATS platforms scan for exact phrases. If the posting says "financial analysis" — use those exact words. Both acronym and full form: "AI" and "Artificial Intelligence".

📐

No Tables, Columns or Graphics

Two-column formats, text boxes, and images confuse ATS parsers. Use a clean single-column layout. Fancy = filtered. Simple = seen.

🏷️

Use Standard Section Headings

Stick to "Work Experience", "Education", "Skills", "Certifications". Creative headings like "My Journey" won't be recognized by the system.

📁

Save as .DOCX or Simple PDF

Some ATS systems struggle with complex PDFs. When in doubt, .docx wins. Name your file: FirstName_LastName_Role.pdf — not "final_v3_new.pdf".

🎯

Tailor Every Single Application

One generic resume won't pass many ATS systems. Customize for each role. 30 minutes of tailoring is worth more than 30 applications with the same resume.

📏

One Page (Freshers) / Two Max (Exp.)

Recruiters spend 6 seconds. Freshers: 1 page always. Under 5 years exp: 1–2 pages. 10+ years: 2 pages, never 3. Every line must earn its spot.

FOR FRESHERS 🎓

Your First Resume:
The 7-Step Playbook

Zero experience isn't zero value. Here's how to build a resume that makes recruiters stop scrolling — even without a single full-time job.

🚀 Build Your Resume Free →
STEP 01
Write a Power Summary — Not an Objective
Ditch the old-school "seeking a position to grow" objective. Write 2–3 lines that say who you are, what you bring, and what role you target. Example: "CS graduate skilled in Python and React. Built 3 live projects including an AI-powered chatbot. Seeking a backend developer role at a product-first company."
🔥 MOST SKIPPED STEP
STEP 02
Let Your Projects Be Your Portfolio
For freshers, strong projects can outweigh work experience. Include hackathon projects, academic capstone work, open-source contributions, college event projects. For each: Project name → Tech stack → What it does → Impact/users/metrics → GitHub link.
⭐ RECRUITER FAVORITE
STEP 03
Quantify Everything — Even Small Wins
"Improved app performance" → "Reduced API response time by 40%". "Managed social media" → "Grew Instagram following by 2,400 in 3 months". Numbers create credibility. If you don't have numbers, estimate conservatively.
📊 INSTANTLY UPGRADES
STEP 04
List Both Hard and Soft Skills Strategically
Technical skills first (Python, SQL, Figma, etc.) — these get ATS matches. Then soft skills, but never list them as words alone. Prove them: "Communication — Led 15-person college event team, managing vendors and logistics."
STEP 05
Add Certifications, Courses & Achievements
Google, Coursera, NPTEL, Udemy, AWS — these signal hustle. LeetCode rankings, hackathon wins, scholarships, NSS leadership? Include them all. A "Certified" label beside your name builds instant credibility.
✅ ATS BOOSTER
STEP 06
Tailor for Every Role — Not Just Once
Don't send the same resume for a frontend dev role and a data analyst role. You need role-specific versions. Tailor the summary, skills, and project highlights for each application. It takes 20 mins and 3× your response rate.
🎯 GAME CHANGER
STEP 07
Keep It to One Page. Always.
As a fresher, you have no reason to exceed one page. Recruiters decide in 6 seconds. Every word must earn its place. Use BanaoResume's free ATS templates — designed specifically to fit a powerful fresher resume on exactly one page.
FOR EXPERIENCED PROS 💼

You Have Experience.
Now Make It Impossible to Ignore.

Mid-career job hunting is a different game. Recruiters assume competence — what they're hunting for is impact. Here's how to show it.

💥

Lead Every Bullet With Impact

Not "Responsible for managing teams" — but "Led 12-person cross-functional team delivering a ₹2Cr product launch 3 weeks ahead of schedule." Action verb + team/scale + result. The formula that makes hiring managers pause mid-scroll.

⏱️

Pass the 6-Second Test

Your name, current/target role, and one powerful achievement must be visible in the top third of page 1. Don't bury the lead. Recruiters decide within seconds whether to read on — structure your resume so the best stuff is inescapable.

🧠

Skills-First Summary Block

Open with a 3-line professional summary that includes your specialisation, years of experience, and key achievement metric. Then a 6-10 keyword skills block. This section alone determines your ATS score — treat it like paid search ad copy.

🔗

Align LinkedIn Perfectly

Job seekers with fully aligned LinkedIn profiles are 71% more likely to land interviews. Your title, dates, and skills must match exactly across resume and LinkedIn. Even small discrepancies trigger ATS red flags — and recruiter suspicion.

📈

Quantify 80% of Your Bullets

"Improved customer satisfaction" means nothing. "Raised NPS from 38 to 67 over 2 quarters by redesigning onboarding flow" means everything. Numbers create credibility, scale, and proof. Aim for at least 8 out of 10 bullets to have a metric.

📝

Two Pages Max — Make Both Count

Under 5 years: 1 page. 5–10 years: 1–2 pages. 10+ years: exactly 2 pages. Cut the early career stuff from 10+ years ago. Recruiters care about the last 5–7 years. A tight, relevant 2-pager beats a bloated 4-pager every single time.

DIGITAL PRESENCE

LinkedIn: Your Resume's
Supercharged Twin

Your LinkedIn profile isn't just a digital resume — it's where recruiters verify, explore, and decide. Here's how to make yours a lead magnet.

71% more interview chances with an aligned profile

Make LinkedIn Work While You Sleep

With over 1 billion users and 58 million companies on LinkedIn, it's where recruiters hunt. Your profile is viewed 10–20 times before every interview. Every element sends a signal.

🎯

Headline Formula That Works

Don't write your job title alone. Use: [Role] | [Skill] | [Achievement/Niche]. Example: "Backend Dev | Python · AWS | Built systems serving 100K+ users". Recruiters search keywords — be discoverable.

📝

Your "About" Section = Cover Letter

The About section (2,000 chars) is the most important on your profile. Tell your story: who you are, what you've achieved, what you want next. End with a clear call-to-action: "Open to [role type] opportunities — DM me."

🔗

Custom LinkedIn URL

Change "linkedin.com/in/user1234" to "linkedin.com/in/yourname". It looks professional on your resume, in emails, and in Google searches. Takes 30 seconds. Do it now.

🤝

Personalize Every Request

Generic connection requests get ignored. Mention where you found them, a mutual interest, or a specific reason. 3–4 personalized sentences dramatically increase acceptance rates.

🔑

Keyword-Rich Skills Section

LinkedIn's AI search matches recruiters to candidates via skills. Add all relevant skills. Get endorsements from colleagues. Recruiters in 2025 use AI-powered search — your skills section is your ranking algorithm.

📸

Profile Picture = First Impression

A professional headshot on a plain background. No sunglasses, group photos, or selfies. Your profile picture is "cemented in hiring managers' minds" — it colours their attitude more than you think.

⚠️ RESUME KILLERS

7 Mistakes That Are
Silently Destroying Your Chances

Recruiters see thousands of resumes. These are the patterns that make them click "No" in under 3 seconds — fix them before your next application.

01

The Generic Objective Statement

"Seeking a challenging position to utilize my skills in a reputed organization" — every recruiter has seen this 10,000 times. It signals zero research and zero effort.

✓ FIX: Write a 2-line power summary with your exact target role and best achievement
02

Saving as "resume_final_v3_NEW.pdf"

The file name is the first thing recruiters see in their inbox or ATS system. This immediately signals disorganization. A small thing with big consequences.

✓ FIX: Save as FirstName_LastName_TargetRole.pdf — always
03

Listing Duties Instead of Achievements

"Responsible for managing social media accounts" tells recruiters nothing. What was the result? Without outcomes, your bullets are just a job description copy-paste.

✓ FIX: Action verb + scale + result. Every single bullet.
04

Mismatched LinkedIn & Resume Details

Job titles, dates, company names that differ between your resume and LinkedIn are instant red flags. 75% of ATS rejections happen due to these inconsistencies.

✓ FIX: Update both together. Check dates, titles, and skills for exact alignment
05

Overusing Buzzwords Without Proof

"Detail-oriented, hardworking team player with excellent communication skills" — every single word here is meaningless without evidence. Recruiters are desensitized to these.

✓ FIX: Replace buzzwords with specific examples and metrics that prove them
06

Two-Column or Graphic-Heavy Formats

They look great in Canva. They look like garbage in an ATS. Tables, text boxes, columns, and icons confuse parsers — your entire resume might be read as blank.

✓ FIX: Use clean single-column ATS-safe templates. Like BanaoResume's free ones.
07

Sending the Same Resume Everywhere

One resume for 50 jobs = 50 ATS rejections. Every role has different keywords, priorities, and formats. A tailored resume 3× your response rate. It takes 20 minutes per application. It's worth it.

✓ FIX: Keep a master resume. Create tailored versions for each job cluster.
