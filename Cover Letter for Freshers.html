Examples

A fresher cover letter example

Built entirely around education and one project — no work history required.

Fresher ExampleBR

Dear Hiring Manager,

I'm a recent BCA graduate applying for the Software Developer Trainee role at [Company]. During my final year, I built a full-stack library management system using Java and MySQL, which strengthened my understanding of real-world application design.

I'm especially interested in [Company] because of [specific reason]. I'm confident my strong foundation in Java and eagerness to learn would make me a valuable addition to your team.

Thank you for considering my application.

Sincerely,
[Your Name]

Mistakes

Common fresher cover letter mistakes

✕

Opening with "I have no experience, but..."

This undersells you immediately — lead with what you do have instead.

✕

Being too generic about the company

"I want to work at a reputed organization" reads as copy-pasted — name something specific.

✕

Overusing words like "passionate" with no proof

Back up enthusiasm with a specific project, course, or action, not just adjectives.

Pro tips

Pro tips for freshers writing cover letters

Treat coursework as real experience

A strong class project can carry as much weight as a short internship if described well.

Research one specific detail about the company

Even a single specific line about the company shows more effort than most fresher applicants make.

Keep the tone confident, not apologetic

Frame your background as ready and eager, not lacking.

FAQ

Cover letter for freshers — FAQ

How do I write a cover letter with no work experience?

Lead with your education and use a project, internship, or coursework as your proof point instead of a job.

Should freshers still write a cover letter?

Yes — a well-written cover letter can meaningfully differentiate a fresher application from others with similar resumes.

How long should a fresher's cover letter be?

2-3 short paragraphs are enough — freshers don't need to fill a full page.
