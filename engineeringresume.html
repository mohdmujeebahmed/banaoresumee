→ What recruiters look for in an engineering resume

What recruiters look for in an engineering resume

Engineering recruiters scan for named tools, quantified project specs, and hands-on testing experience.

01

Specific software named

SolidWorks, AutoCAD, MATLAB — not generic "CAD software."

02

A design project with real specs

Tolerances, loads, or materials show engineering rigor beyond a class summary.

03

Evidence of testing and iteration

"Designed, prototyped, and tested" beats "designed a part" alone.

→ Mistakes

Common engineering resume mistakes

✕

Saying "CAD software" instead of naming it

Recruiters and ATS scan for SolidWorks, AutoCAD, or Fusion 360 by name.

✕

No testing or iteration mentioned

Show you tested and refined your design, not just designed it once.

✕

Skipping units and tolerances

Specific numbers signal real engineering rigor, not a class summary.

→ Pro tips

Pro tips for engineering resumes

Name every tool used

List each specific software, not a general category.

Quantify design impact

Cut time, reduced cost, improved efficiency — attach a number.

Link a portfolio if available

A project portfolio or video demo adds proof beyond text.

→ FAQ

Engineering resume — FAQ

What should an engineering resume include?

Core tools (CAD/MATLAB), a specced project, internship experience, and quantified results — kept to one page for freshers.

Do I need a portfolio for an engineering resume?

It helps but isn't required — a strong project section with results can stand alone.

What's the best format for an engineering resume?

Reverse-chronological with a projects section highlighted near the top for freshers.
