How TCS Actually Screens You

The TCS Resume Pipeline — What Happens After You Apply

Unlike product companies, TCS resume screening is largely rule-based before a human ever reads it. Here's the real sequence.

1

TCS iON / Portal Parsing

Your resume/profile is parsed into structured fields — percentages, skills, backlog status — before any manual review.

2

Eligibility Filtering

Candidates below the 60%/6.0 CGPA cutoff (10th, 12th, degree) or with active backlogs are auto-filtered at this stage.

3

NQT / Assessment

Shortlisted profiles move to the National Qualifier Test — aptitude, verbal, and coding sections decide the next round.

4

Interview Rounds

Technical + Managerial + HR rounds — where your resume is finally read line-by-line by a human panel.

Pick Your Track

Tailor Your Resume to the TCS Hiring Band You're Targeting

TCS hires under different tracks with different expectations. Your resume should shift emphasis depending on which one you're applying for.

NINJA

For Freshers (0–1 yr)

Entry-level track. Focus on fundamentals and academic performance.

  • Lead with education % / CGPA
  • Highlight core CS subjects (DBMS, OOPs, OS)
  • 1–2 academic or mini projects
  • Any certification (NPTEL, Coursera) helps
DIGITAL

For Tech-Skilled Candidates

For candidates with strong project/tech exposure — cloud, full-stack, data.

  • Lead with tech stack, not just education
  • 2–3 real, demoable projects with GitHub links
  • Mention specific tools: React, AWS, Python, etc.
  • Internship experience carries extra weight
PRIME

For Experienced Hires

Lateral hiring track — resume should read like a professional, not a fresher.

  • Lead with current role + years of experience
  • Quantify impact in each role (%, scale, savings)
  • Domain expertise called out clearly (BFSI, Retail, etc.)
  • Certifications relevant to the role (AWS, PMP, etc.)

Why Resumes Get Rejected

Real Reasons TCS Resumes Get Filtered Out

Based on common patterns in TCS off-campus and NQT rejections.

60%
Cutoff

Missing or unclear academic percentages

If 10th, 12th, or degree scores aren't clearly listed with numbers, automated filtering can reject the profile outright — even if you're eligible.

Gaps

Unexplained education or employment gaps

A 1-2 year gap with no context (certification, backlog clearance, family reason) raises questions recruiters won't chase — they'll just skip to the next resume.

Fancy

Overdesigned templates

Multi-column layouts, icons for skill ratings, and graphics often break parsing on TCS's system — plain, clean formatting performs better here.

Generic

Same resume sent for Ninja, Digital, and Prime

Recruiters can tell when a resume isn't tailored — a Digital-track resume that reads like a pure fresher resume (or vice versa) hurts your fit score.

Interview Prep

What to Prepare for Each TCS Interview Round

Round 1

Technical Interview

  • Be ready to explain every project on your resume in detail
  • Core subjects: OOPs, DBMS, OS basics are almost always asked
  • Simple coding questions (loops, arrays, strings)
Round 2

Managerial Round

  • Expect scenario questions: "How would you handle a tight deadline?"
  • They assess client-facing readiness, not just technical depth
  • Be honest about strengths/weaknesses — over-polished answers stand out negatively
Round 3

HR Interview

  • "Why TCS?" — have a genuine, specific answer ready
  • Relocation and shift-flexibility questions are common — answer clearly
  • Salary/bond related questions — know TCS's standard fresher terms beforehand
FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What resume format does TCS accept?

A simple, single-column PDF resume, ideally 1 page for freshers, with career objective, skills, projects, and clearly listed education percentages/CGPA.

Does TCS use ATS to filter resumes?

Yes — TCS iON and its internal ATS parse resumes before a recruiter sees them, so avoid tables, images, and multi-column layouts.

Is CGPA mandatory on a TCS resume?

Yes, since shortlisting is criteria-based for freshers — always list 10th, 12th, and degree percentage/CGPA clearly.
