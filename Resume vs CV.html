How a resume and CV actually differ

How a resume and CV actually differ

The core difference is scope and purpose, not just naming convention.

01

Length and depth

A resume is a concise 1-2 page summary; a CV is a comprehensive, often multi-page record of your entire career and academic history.

02

Regional usage

In the US and Canada, "resume" is standard for most jobs; in the UK, India, and much of Europe, "CV" is often used interchangeably with resume for standard job applications.

03

Purpose and audience

Resumes target specific jobs; CVs are standard for academic, research, medical, and some international job applications.

Mistakes

Common resume vs CV mistakes

✕

Submitting a CV when a resume was requested

A multi-page CV for a corporate job application often reads as unfocused — trim to a resume instead.

✕

Using a short resume for academic applications

Academic and research roles typically expect a comprehensive CV, not a condensed 1-page resume.

✕

Assuming the terms mean the same thing everywhere

Regional differences matter — always check what's expected for the specific country and industry.

Pro tips

Pro tips for choosing the right format

Check the job posting's country

Confirm whether resume or CV is standard for that country and role type before applying.

Default to resume for most corporate jobs

Unless academia, research, or a country-specific norm applies, a concise resume is usually correct.

Keep both versions ready if needed

If you apply across academia and industry, maintain a resume and a full CV as separate documents.

FAQ

Resume vs CV — FAQ

Is a CV longer than a resume?

Yes — a CV is typically a comprehensive, multi-page document, while a resume is a concise 1-2 page summary.

Which should I use for a corporate job?

A resume is standard for most corporate job applications in the US, Canada, and increasingly worldwide.

When is a CV required instead of a resume?

Academic, research, medical, and some international roles typically require a full CV rather than a condensed resume.
