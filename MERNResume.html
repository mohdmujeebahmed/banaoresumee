→ Common mistakes

Common MERN resume mistakes to avoid

✕

No live deployed link

A GitHub repo alone isn't enough — recruiters want a clickable live demo of your MERN project.

✕

Listing "MERN stack" with no specifics

Break it down — name React, Node, Express, and MongoDB individually so ATS keyword matching works.

✕

No mention of state management

Leaving out Redux or Context API makes your frontend experience look basic to recruiters scanning for depth.

✕

Overloading the resume with every JS library used

Stick to the stack that matters for the role — a cluttered skills list dilutes your strongest, most relevant skills.

→ Interview prep

Common interview questions for MERN developers

"Walk me through your MERN project"

Cover architecture, your role, and one metric (users, requests, load time) in under 90 seconds.

"Explain how JWT authentication works"

Be ready to explain tokens, middleware, and protected routes with a simple diagram in words.

"Redux vs Context API?"

Know the trade-offs — Redux for larger apps with complex state, Context for smaller, simpler state needs.

MongoDB schema design

Expect questions on embedding vs referencing documents — practice with a real example from your project.
