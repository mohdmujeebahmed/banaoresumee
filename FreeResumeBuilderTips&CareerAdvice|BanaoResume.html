⚡ Quick Resume Tips That Actually Work

Evidence-backed tips from recruiters at top companies

Tip 01 — Contact Info

Add LinkedIn URL & GitHub

Resumes with a complete LinkedIn link get 40% more recruiter responses. Match your headline to your resume title exactly.

Tip 02 — Summary

Write a 3-line summary, not an objective

Objective statements are outdated. Show who you are, what you've done, and what value you bring — in under 50 words.

Tip 03 — Experience

Use the X-Y-Z formula for bullets

"Accomplished [X] as measured by [Y] by doing [Z]." Example: Increased sales by 32% in Q3 by launching a targeted email campaign.

Tip 04 — ATS

Mirror exact keywords from job description

Copy the job description into a word-frequency tool. The top nouns and verbs are your ATS keywords. Include them naturally.

Tip 05 — Length

Keep it to 1 page (under 10 years)

Unless you're a senior leader, one page is gold standard. Use 0.5" margins, 10-11pt font, cut anything older than 10 years.

Tip 06 — Format

Always submit PDF unless told otherwise

PDFs preserve formatting across devices. Name your file: FirstName-LastName-Resume.pdf for a professional touch.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long should a resume be in 2025?
For most job seekers with under 10 years of experience, one page is ideal. Senior professionals may use two pages if every line adds value. The rule: include only what helps you get the interview.
What is an ATS-friendly resume?
An ATS friendly resume uses simple formatting, standard headings, and job-specific keywords. Avoid tables, text boxes, headers/footers, and graphics. BanaoResume templates are all ATS-tested.
Should I include a photo on my resume?
In India, a professional photo is commonly accepted. In the US, UK, Canada, and Australia, photos are typically omitted to prevent unconscious bias. Follow norms of the country you're applying to.
How do I write a resume with no experience?
Focus on transferable skills, academic projects, internships, certifications, and volunteer work. Use a skills-based format. Lead with a strong summary that highlights your potential.
What font should I use on my resume?
Use ATS-readable fonts like Calibri (11pt), Arial (11pt), or Garamond (11pt). Avoid decorative fonts. Use 10.5–12pt for body, 14–16pt for your name at the top.
How do I tailor my resume for each job?
Read the job description and mirror the exact language used for required skills. Adjust your summary to address the specific role. This takes 15 minutes but can double your response rate.
