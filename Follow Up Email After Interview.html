Mistakes

Common follow-up email mistakes

✕

Sending it too late

A follow-up sent more than 2-3 days later loses most of its intended impact.

✕

Making it too long or repetitive

Keep it brief — restating your entire resume in the follow-up is unnecessary and can feel excessive.

✕

Following up too many times afterward

One follow-up after the interview, and one more after a reasonable wait, is generally sufficient.

Pro tips

Pro tips for interview follow-up emails

Send it the same day or next morning

Prompt timing shows genuine enthusiasm and keeps you fresh in the interviewer's mind.

Reference something specific from the conversation

A real detail makes the email feel personal rather than templated.

Keep it under 100 words

Brief, warm, and specific outperforms long and generic every time.

FAQ

Follow up email after interview — FAQ

When should I send a follow-up email after an interview?

Within 24 hours of the interview, ideally the same day or the next morning.

What should I include in the follow-up email?

A brief thank you, a specific detail from the conversation, and a reaffirmation of your interest in the role.

Is it okay to follow up if I haven't heard back?

Yes — a polite follow-up after a week or two of silence is generally acceptable and shows continued interest.
