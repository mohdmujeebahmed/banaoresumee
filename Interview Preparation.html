What interviewers are actually evaluating

What interviewers are actually evaluating

Beyond your answers, interviewers are consistently assessing a few underlying signals.

01

Clarity of communication

How clearly and concisely you explain your experience often matters as much as the experience itself.

02

Structured thinking under pressure

The STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result) shows organized thinking, not just a good story.

03

Genuine interest in the specific role

Vague, generic answers to "why this company" are one of the most common red flags for interviewers.

Examples

Common interview questions and how to approach them

Frameworks for the questions that come up in nearly every interview.

"Tell me about yourself"Lead with your current role or degree, one key achievement, and what you're looking for next — under 60 seconds.
"Tell me about a time you failed"Pick a real, contained failure. Spend most of your answer on what you changed afterward, not the failure itself.
"Why do you want to work here?"Name one specific product, team, or value from your research — avoid generic answers like "great culture."
Mistakes

Common interview mistakes

✕

Giving vague, unstructured answers

Without a clear structure like STAR, even strong experiences can sound disorganized to an interviewer.

✕

Not preparing questions to ask

Having no questions for the interviewer often reads as low genuine interest in the role.

✕

Skipping company research entirely

Generic answers about "growth" or "opportunity" stand out poorly against candidates who did their homework.

Pro tips

Pro tips for interview day

Prepare 3-4 flexible STAR stories

Most behavioral questions can be answered by adapting the same few strong stories.

Practice with a timer

Keep most answers under 90 seconds — concise answers read as more confident and prepared.

Send a follow-up within 24 hours

A brief, genuine thank-you note keeps you memorable and shows professionalism.

FAQ

Interview preparation — FAQ

What is the STAR method?

A framework for answering behavioral questions: Situation, Task, Action, Result — used to structure clear, complete answers.

How many mock interviews should I do?

2-3 mock interviews with a peer or mentor significantly improve clarity and confidence over reading answers silently.

What should I research before an interview?

The company's products, recent news, the specific role, and if possible, your interviewer's background.
