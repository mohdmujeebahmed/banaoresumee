FAQ & Resume Tips

Everything you need to know about building a winning resume 🎯

General Questions

Is BanaoResume really 100% free?+
Yes, absolutely. No credit card, no sign-up, no hidden charges.
Is my data safe?+
All resume data is processed entirely in your browser and never sent to our servers. Read our Privacy Policy for full details.
Does BanaoResume work on mobile phones?+
Yes, the builder is fully responsive. For downloading as PDF, a desktop browser (Chrome or Edge) gives the cleanest results.

The IIT-to-Google Format

What makes the IIT-to-Google format special?+
This is the classic single-column resume format that IIT, NIT, and BITS placement cells have taught for decades. It uses a centered name header, bold section titles with full-width horizontal rules, and clean black text — no columns, no colors. It's the format that has historically gotten Indian engineers into Google, Microsoft, Amazon, and other top companies because it's 100% ATS-safe and human-readable.
Should I use IIT-to-Google format or Modern Orange?+
Use IIT-to-Google Format if you're applying to product companies, MNCs, or any company with a strict ATS (Google, Microsoft, Amazon, Goldman Sachs, McKinsey, etc.). Use Modern Orange for startups, design-forward companies, or roles where standing out visually helps (UI/UX, product, growth). Use ATS Minimal when you want the safest B&W print.

ATS & Resume Formatting

What is ATS and why does it matter?+
ATS (Applicant Tracking System) is software used by most companies to auto-screen resumes. If your resume has wrong fonts, tables, or images, it gets filtered out automatically. All BanaoResume templates use clean, ATS-readable formatting. Read our full ATS guide →
Should my resume be one page or two pages?+
For freshers and candidates with under 5 years of experience: stick to one page. Recruiters spend 6–10 seconds scanning a resume. BanaoResume's A4 template is optimised for a clean one-page layout.

Freshers & Students

I have no work experience. What do I put on my resume?+
Focus on: (1) Projects — personal, academic, or open source; (2) Internships; (3) Skills; (4) Education; (5) Achievements. Recruiters expect freshers to have light experience sections — your projects tell the real story.
How do I write project descriptions that impress recruiters?+
Use the "What + How + Impact" formula: What did you build? How (tech stack)? Impact — users, metrics, problem solved. Example: "Built a real-time expense tracker using React and Firebase serving 200+ beta users, reducing manual logging time by 60%."
